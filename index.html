Intro on our motivations

Both interest and need for sustainable and usable public transportation systems are growing. Transportation is a key part of student life, as well as city and campus planning. Walk times, shuttles, buses, trains, and bikes are all transportation systems that impact the day to day life of students, residents, and college administrators. We chose to focus on biking, since the city bike organization for Boston, Bluebikes, publishes its data and encourages analysis of the data and creation of data visualizations. We chose Bluebikes specifically, because the data and analysis is relevant to us students; in fact, two of the members of this team actively use Bluebikes. Cambridge is a particularly bike-friendly city in terms of terrain and climate, which should yield interesting data that can inform the implementation of bike sharing programs in other cities. In our analysis of BLUEbike usage, we have focused on identifying trends in usage around college campuses. We have chosen Harvard and MIT campuses specifically as representative institutions. We also analyze only 2018-2019 data, which includes more detailed demographic information along with the rides data.
