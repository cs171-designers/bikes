Intro on our motivations

Both interest and need for sustainable and usable public transportation systems are growing. Transportation is a key part of student life, as well as city and campus planning. Walk times, shuttles, buses, trains, and bikes are all transportation systems that impact the day to day life of students, residents, and college administrators.
We chose to focus on biking, since the city bike organization for Boston, Bluebikes, publishes its data and encourages analysis of the data and creation of data visualizations. We chose Bluebikes specifically, because the data and analysis is relevant to us students; in fact, two of the members of this team actively use Bluebikes. Cambridge is a particularly bike-friendly city in terms of terrain and climate, which should yield interesting data that can inform the implementation of bike sharing programs in other cities.
In our analysis of BLUEbike usage, we have focused on identifying trends in usage on Harvard campus specifically. We are interested in exploring where on campus, including both the undergraduate and graduate schools, are most frequently visited using BlueBikes and what the typical user profile is like. We also analyze only 2018-2019 data, which includes more detailed demographic information along with the rides data.

Let's follow a bike! Understanding BLUEbikes

Welcome to Boston! Here is a map that follows a bike around the city. We will go on a virtual ride so that even if you have never borrowed a Bluebike, you can understand the process of how a bike may be checked out from one station, ridden for a certain amount of time, and then returned to another station; then another user may borrow the bike from that station, and the cycle continues.
BlueBike users may be subscription members or customers who pay for each trip.
Click "next" to move from one station along the trip to another. You can also select a different bike to follow!
