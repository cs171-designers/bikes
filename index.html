Intro on our motivations

Both interest and need for sustainable and usable public transportation systems are growing. Transportation is a key part of student life, as well as city and campus planning. Walk times, shuttles, buses, trains, and bikes are all transportation systems that impact the day-to-day life of students, residents, and college administrators.

We chose to explore biking, since the city bike organization for Boston, Bluebikes, publishes its data and encourages analysis of the data and creation of data visualizations. We chose Bluebikes specifically, because the data and analysis is relevant to us students; in fact, two of the members of this team actively use Bluebikes. Cambridge is a particularly bike-friendly city in terms of terrain and climate, which should yield interesting data that can inform the implementation of bike sharing programs in other cities.

In our analysis of BLUEbike usage, we have focused on identifying trends in usage on Harvard campus specifically. We are interested in exploring where on campus, including both the undergraduate and graduate schools, are most frequently visited using BlueBikes and what the typical user profile is like. We also analyze only 2018-2019 data, which includes more detailed demographic information along with the rides data.

Ridership trends

Beyond understanding where you fit in with the data, let's take a broader look at ridership trends over time and by demographics.

As you can see, ridership tends to increase in the summer months and decrease in the winter.

When looking at the rides broken down by various demographics, we see that subscribers, males, and adults (25+) tend to complete the most rides.

These pie charts further break down the demographics into more detailed age groups.

From these pie charts, we see that most BLUEbike users are Males, then Females. Users with unspecified gender are likely customers who were not registered members, so their demographic information is unavailable.

We might notice that there is more male rides than female rides. However, it is important to note that these are the number of rides and not the number of users (because users are anonymized). This invites questions of whether this is due to a difference in the number of users by sex or the number of average rides per user by sex.

Looking at the age chart, there are a couple insights. First we see that the number of rides for ages 28-38 is close to or greater than the number of rides for ages 18-28. This is interesting because we are looking at rides centered around the Harvard campus.

We can infer that there is probably a low percentage of undergraduate users. These stations are centered around Harvard which combined have an undergraduate population of almost 10 thousand. If we divide the number of rides by 10k, and consider the time frame we see that the number of rides is quite low.

Trip Duration trends

Although we know that Males, Subscribers, and Adults (mostly 18-38) constitute a majority of the rides, how does the average trip duration of each ride compare across demographic categories?

[[INSERT FORM]] Do you expect subscribers or non-sbuscribers to take longer trips on average?

Although subscribers take more rides than non-subscribers, these rides are actually shorter (by about 15 minutes) on average than rides by non-subscribing customers! This could be because subscribers are already paying for unlimited rides and feel that it is worth the money to indulge in shorter rides.

Following the same trend, users with unknown gender are likely non-subscribing customers where their demographic data is not entered. So, they also tend to take longer rides along the same trend as the customers line in the previous graph. However, we also see that Male and Female riders tend to take trips that are about the same duration, although trips by Females are slightly longer.

When examining trip duration by age, we see that Young adult and adult users take trips of very similar average duration, around 15 minutes long. However, young riders under 18 have a much more variation in trip duration.

Conclusions

from this data, we see that the most BLUEbike rides on Harvard’s campus are completed by subscribers, males, and adults 25+. Subscribers take on average 15 minute shorter rides. Females take slightly longer rides than males. Youth have a lot of variation in trip duration, though young adults and adults tend to take trips that are about 15 minutes long.
